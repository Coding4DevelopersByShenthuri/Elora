**Category**: Business English

### [00:30 - 01:15] FORMAL GREETINGS - Part 1
**Speaker**: [Sitting at desk, professional setting]
"Let's start with formal greetings. In business settings, you want to sound professional and confident.

Here are the most common formal greetings:

'Good morning' - used before noon
'Good afternoon' - used from noon until about 5 PM  
'Good evening' - used after 5 PM

Notice how we say 'Good morning' with emphasis on 'Good' and 'morning'. Let's practice this together."

### [02:00 - 02:45] INTRODUCING YOURSELF
**Speaker**: [Standing, confident posture]
"Now, let's learn how to introduce yourself professionally.

The most common phrase is:
'I'm [Your Name]. It's a pleasure to meet you.'

Or slightly more formal:
'Hello, I'm [Your Name]. Nice to meet you.'

Watch my facial expression - I'm making eye contact and smiling. This is important in professional settings."

**[INTERACTION POINT: Speaking Exercise #3]**

### [02:45 - 03:30] PRACTICE DIALOGUE

**Person A**: 'Good morning!'

Notice how both people maintain eye contact and speak clearly. Let's practice this dialogue together."

**[INTERACTION POINT: Speaking Exercise #4 - Dialogue]**

### [03:30 - 04:15] SAYING GOODBYE PROFESSIONALLY
**Speaker**: [Back at desk]
"When ending a professional conversation, you have several options:

For formal situations:
'It was a pleasure meeting you.'
'Thank you for your time.'
'Have a great day.'

For slightly less formal:
'Nice meeting you!'
'Take care!'
'See you soon!'

Let's practice the formal goodbye."

**[INTERACTION POINT: Speaking Exercise #5]**
**Timestamp**: 04:00
**Type**: Repeat After
**Phrase**: "It was a pleasure meeting you. Have a great day!"
**Duration**: 0 (pause until completed)

---

### [04:15 - 04:45] COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
**Speaker**: [Direct to camera, helpful tone]
"Let me share common mistakes learners make:

**Mistake 1**: Saying 'Hi' in very formal settings
**Better**: Use 'Hello' or 'Good morning'

**Mistake 2**: Speaking too fast
**Better**: Speak slowly and clearly

**Mistake 3**: Forgetting to smile
**Better**: A warm smile shows confidence

Remember, in professional settings, clarity and confidence matter more than speed."

**Receptionist**: 'Nice to meet you, Mr. Smith. Please take a seat.'

Notice the professional tone and clear pronunciation."

**[INTERACTION POINT: Speaking Exercise #6 - Full Dialogue]**

### [05:15 - 05:45] SUMMARY & KEY TAKEAWAYS
**Speaker**: [Back to main desk, summarizing]
"Let's recap what we learned today:

**Key Takeaways**:
1. Use time-appropriate greetings: Good morning, afternoon, or evening
2. Introduce yourself clearly: 'I'm [Name]. Nice to meet you.'
3. End professionally: 'It was a pleasure meeting you.'
4. Maintain eye contact and speak clearly
5. Match formality to the situation

You now have the foundation for professional greetings in English!"
