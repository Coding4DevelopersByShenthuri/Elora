### [00:40 - 01:45] ANSWERING BUSINESS CALLS
**Speaker**: [Demonstrating phone etiquette]
"Let's start with answering business calls. The first 10 seconds set the tone.

**Professional Greetings**:
'[Company Name], this is [Your Name]. How can I help you?'
'Good morning, [Company Name], [Your Name] speaking. How may I assist you?'
'[Company Name], [Your Name]. What can I do for you?'

Notice three key elements: Company name, your name, and offer to help.

**Slightly More Formal**:
'Hello, this is [Your Name] from [Company Name]. How can I help you today?'

Practice these greetings - they sound professional and confident."

### [01:45 - 02:50] MAKING OUTGOING CALLS
**Speaker": [Making a call gesture, professional]
"When making outgoing calls, you need to identify yourself clearly.

**Professional Opening**:
'Hello, this is [Your Name] from [Company Name]. I'm calling regarding [reason].'
'Good afternoon, this is [Your Name] calling from [Company Name]. Is [Name] available?'
'Hi, this is [Your Name]. I'd like to speak with [Name], please.'

**Requesting Connection**:
'Could I speak with [Name], please?'
'Is [Name] available?'
'May I speak to [Name]?'

**Stating Your Purpose**:
'I'm calling about [topic].'
'I'd like to discuss [topic].'
'I need to speak with you regarding [topic].'

Always state your name and company first, then your purpose."

### [02:50 - 03:50] CONFIRMING INFORMATION
**Speaker": [Checking notes, demonstrating]
"During phone calls, you often need to confirm information. Here are essential phrases:

**Asking to Repeat**:
'I'm sorry, could you repeat that?'
'Could you say that again, please?'
'I didn't catch that. Could you repeat it?'

**Confirming Details**:
'Just to confirm, [information]. Is that correct?'
'Let me repeat that back to you: [information].'
'So, you're saying [information]. Is that right?'

**Clarifying**:
'Could you clarify what you mean by [word/phrase]?'
'What exactly do you mean by [word/phrase]?'
'Could you be more specific about [topic]?'

**Spelling Out**:
'Could you spell that for me?'
'Let me spell that back: [letters].'

These phrases prevent misunderstandings and show professionalism."

### [03:50 - 05:00] TRANSFERRING CALLS AND MESSAGES
**Speaker": [Demonstrating office phone system]
"Sometimes you need to transfer calls or take messages. Here's how to do it professionally:

**Transferring Calls**:
'I'll transfer you to [Name/Department]. One moment, please.'
'Let me connect you with [Name]. Please hold.'
'That's handled by [Department]. Let me transfer you.'

**Taking Messages**:
'I'm sorry, [Name] is not available right now. Can I take a message?'
'[Name] is in a meeting. Would you like me to take a message?'
'[Name] is away from their desk. Can I have them call you back?'

**Getting Message Details**:
'What's your name, please?'
'What's your phone number?'
'What's the message regarding?'
'When is a good time to call you back?'

**Confirming Message**:
'Let me read that back: [message details]. Is that correct?'
'So the message is: [details]. Is that right?'

Always confirm message details to ensure accuracy."

### [05:00 - 06:00] HANDLING DIFFICULT SITUATIONS
**Speaker": [Calm, professional demeanor]
"Phone calls can present challenges. Here's how to handle them professionally:

**When You Can't Hear Clearly**:
'I'm sorry, the line is breaking up. Could you repeat that?'
'I'm having trouble hearing you. Could you speak a bit louder?'
'We seem to have a bad connection. Could you call back?'

**When You Need More Time**:
'Let me check on that for you. Could you hold for a moment?'
'I need to verify that information. Can I call you back in [time]?'
'Let me look into that and get back to you.'

**When You Need to Call Back**:
'I don't have that information right now. Can I call you back at [time]?'
'Let me get the details and call you back. What's a good time?'
'I'll need to check on that. When would be convenient to call you?'

Always be honest, professional, and follow through on promises."

### [06:00 - 06:50] ENDING CALLS PROFESSIONALLY
**Speaker": [Professional, warm]
"Ending calls professionally leaves a positive impression. Here are effective closings:

**Standard Closings**:
'Thank you for calling. Have a great day!'
'Is there anything else I can help you with?'
'Thank you. Goodbye!'
'Thanks for your call. Goodbye!'

**More Formal Closings**:
'Thank you for contacting [Company Name]. Have a wonderful day!'
'We appreciate your call. Is there anything else we can assist you with?'
'Thank you for calling. We look forward to speaking with you again!'

**Confirming Next Steps**:
'I'll [action] and call you back at [time].'
'I'll send you [information] by [time/date].'
'Let me follow up with you on [date].'

**For Outgoing Calls**:
'Thank you for your time. Goodbye!'
'I appreciate you taking my call. Have a great day!'
'Thank you. I'll be in touch. Goodbye!'

Always end on a positive, professional note."
