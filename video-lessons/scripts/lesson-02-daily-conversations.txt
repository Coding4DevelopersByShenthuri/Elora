### [00:35 - 01:30] ORDERING COFFEE - Part 1
**Speaker**: [At counter, demonstrating]
"Let's start with ordering coffee. In English-speaking countries, coffee shops use specific language.

Common phrases you'll hear:
'What can I get for you?'
'Would you like anything else?'
'For here or to go?'

And here's how you respond:
'I'd like a [coffee type], please.'
'Can I have a [coffee type], please?'
'Could I get a [coffee type]?'

Notice the polite words: 'please', 'could', 'would'. These make your English sound natural and polite."

### [01:30 - 02:30] COFFEE VOCABULARY
**Speaker**: [Pointing to menu board, visual aid]
"Let's learn common coffee shop vocabulary:

**Coffee Types**:
- Cappuccino: Espresso with steamed milk and foam
- Latte: Espresso with more milk
- Americano: Espresso with hot water
- Espresso: Strong, concentrated coffee
- Macchiato: Espresso with a little milk foam

**Sizes**:
- Small / Regular
- Medium / Grande
- Large / Venti

Practice saying these words with me."

### [02:30 - 03:30] COMPLETE ORDERING DIALOGUE
**Speaker**: [Role-play with barista]
"Now let's see a complete ordering conversation:

**Barista**: 'Hi! What can I get for you today?'
**Customer**: 'Hi! I'd like a medium cappuccino, please.'
**Barista**: 'For here or to go?'
**Customer**: 'To go, please.'
**Barista**: 'That'll be four dollars. Anything else?'
**Customer**: 'No, that's all. Thank you!'
**Barista**: 'Great! Your coffee will be ready in a minute.'

Notice how the customer uses 'please' and 'thank you'. These make conversations friendly and polite."

### [03:30 - 04:20] MAKING SMALL TALK
**Speaker**: [Sitting at coffee shop table]
"While waiting for your order, you might want to make small talk. Small talk is casual conversation about everyday topics.

Safe topics for small talk:
- The weather: 'Nice day, isn't it?'
- The coffee shop: 'This place is busy today!'
- General comments: 'The coffee here is great!'

Avoid personal questions until you know someone better. Let's practice a small talk exchange."

**[INTERACTION POINT: Speaking Exercise #4]**
**Timestamp**: 04:05

**Phrase**: "Nice day, isn't it?"
**Duration**: 0 (pause until completed)

---

### [04:20 - 05:10] ASKING FOR HELP
**Speaker**: [Demonstrating scenarios]
"Sometimes you need to ask for help or clarification. Here are polite ways to do that:

**Asking to repeat**:
'I'm sorry, could you repeat that?'
'Could you say that again, please?'

**Asking for clarification**:
'What does [word] mean?'
'Could you explain that?'

**Asking for something**:
'Excuse me, could I have...?'
'Could you help me with...?'

Always start with 'Excuse me' or 'I'm sorry' to be polite."

### [06:20 - 06:45] PRACTICE TIPS
**Speaker**: [Encouraging, direct to camera]

4. **Start Simple**: Begin with basic orders, then add complexity
