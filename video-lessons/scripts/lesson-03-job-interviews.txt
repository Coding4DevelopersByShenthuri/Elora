### [00:40 - 01:40] PROFESSIONAL SELF-INTRODUCTION
**Speaker**: [Standing, professional attire]
"Let's start with introducing yourself. In interviews, first impressions matter.

Here's a powerful self-introduction structure:

'Good morning. My name is [Your Name]. I'm a [Your Profession] with [Years] years of experience in [Field]. I'm particularly interested in this position because [Specific Reason].'

For example:
'Good morning. My name is Sarah Chen. I'm a marketing specialist with five years of experience in digital marketing. I'm particularly interested in this position because your company's innovative approach aligns with my career goals.'

Notice the structure: Name, role, experience, interest. This shows confidence and preparation."

### [01:40 - 02:50] DESCRIBING YOUR EXPERIENCE
**Speaker**: [At desk, demonstrating confidence]
"When describing your experience, use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

Here are powerful phrases:

**Starting your answer**:
'In my previous role at [Company]...'
'I have extensive experience in...'
'One example that demonstrates this...'

**Describing achievements**:
'I successfully [achievement]...'
'I was responsible for [responsibility]...'
'This resulted in [outcome]...'

**Quantifying results**:
'I increased sales by 20%.'
'I managed a team of 10 people.'
'I completed [number] projects.'

Let's practice structuring your experience."

### [02:50 - 04:00] ANSWERING COMMON QUESTIONS
**Speaker**: [Direct to camera, reassuring]
"Interviewers often ask predictable questions. Here's how to answer them effectively:

**Question 1: 'Tell me about yourself'**
'Well, I'm a [profession] with a passion for [field]. Over the past [years] years, I've [achievement]. What excites me about this opportunity is [specific reason].'

**Question 2: 'What are your strengths?'**
'One of my key strengths is [strength]. For example, [specific example]. This skill would be valuable in this role because [connection to job].'

**Question 3: 'Why do you want this job?'**
'I'm interested in this position for three reasons. First, [reason 1]. Second, [reason 2]. Finally, [reason 3]. This role aligns perfectly with my career goals.'

Notice how each answer is structured and specific."

### [04:00 - 05:00] HANDLING DIFFICULT QUESTIONS
**Speaker**: [Professional, calm demeanor]
"Sometimes interviewers ask challenging questions. Here's how to handle them:

**Question: 'What is your weakness?'**
Don't say you have no weaknesses. Instead:
'One area I've been working on is [weakness]. However, I've taken steps to improve this by [action]. I see this as an opportunity for growth.'

**Question: 'Why did you leave your last job?'**
Be positive:
'I'm looking for new challenges and growth opportunities. This position offers [specific opportunity] that aligns with my career goals.'

**Question: 'Where do you see yourself in 5 years?'**
Be realistic and aligned:
'I see myself growing within this company, taking on more responsibilities, and contributing to [specific goals].'

Always frame challenges as growth opportunities."

### [05:00 - 06:00] ASKING INTELLIGENT QUESTIONS
**Speaker": [Engaged, thoughtful]
"At the end of interviews, you'll be asked: 'Do you have any questions for us?'

Asking good questions shows interest and intelligence. Here are excellent questions to ask:

**About the Role**:
'What would a typical day look like in this position?'
'What are the biggest challenges facing this role?'
'What success looks like in this position?'

**About the Company**:
'What do you enjoy most about working here?'
'How would you describe the company culture?'
'What are the company's goals for the next year?'

**About Growth**:
'What opportunities for professional development are available?'
'How do you support employee growth and learning?'

Avoid asking about salary, vacation, or benefits until you receive an offer."

### [07:10 - 07:40] PRACTICE TIPS
**Speaker": [Encouraging, direct to camera]
"Here are tips to prepare for your interview:

1. **Practice Out Loud**: Rehearse your answers until they flow naturally
2. **Prepare Examples**: Have 3-5 specific examples ready (STAR method)
3. **Research the Company**: Know about the company before the interview
4. **Practice Body Language**: Eye contact, posture, professional dress
5. **Prepare Questions**: Write down 3-5 questions to ask
6. **Record Yourself**: Practice in front of a mirror or record yourself

Remember, preparation builds confidence. The more you practice, the more natural you'll sound!"
